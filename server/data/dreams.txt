Dreams have fascinated the human mind for thousands of years.
Every night we fall into sleep and enter a world of images and emotions.
Some dreams feel so real that waking up becomes a shock.
The ancient Greeks believed dreams were messages from the gods.
In modern psychology, Freud argued that dreams reveal hidden desires of the unconscious mind.
Jung saw dreams as a bridge between the conscious and unconscious self.
During REM sleep, the brain becomes almost as active as when we are awake.
Scientists have discovered that dreams help consolidate memory and process emotions.
Lucid dreaming is the rare experience of knowing you are inside a dream.
Some people train themselves to control their dreams and explore imaginary worlds.
Nightmares are dreams filled with fear, anxiety, and a sense of dread.
Children experience nightmares more frequently than adults.
Sleep deprivation can cause hallucinations that blur the line between dream and reality.
The average person spends about six years of their life dreaming.
Whether dreams hold deep meaning or are simply random neural activity remains an open question.
